Live Jury Chatroom

Instructions:

In this task, you will be acting as a juror in a legal case.

You will first participate in an online jury selection process where you answer questions from a judge. The jury selection process is a chance for the judge to ask potential jurors questions to ensure they could consider the case fairly. Next, if you are selected for the jury, you will hear brief presentations from the attorneys on both sides of the case, and then discuss the case with your fellow jurors.

Voir dire is a chance for judges and lawyers to ask jurors questions to ensure the jurors are able to consider the case fairly. Normally, jury selection and trials are conducted in person, but in light of COVID-19, courts across the country want to transition to an online format for jury trials. So, we are testing out methods to resolve legal disputes online. This study is vital to advancing how the courts work and improving the functioning of our legal system. So, we need you to participate seriously and honestly. Please treat this as a real jury selection and trial, because this is an actual case that will soon be decided by a real jury, either in person or online. The U.S. court system needs our help to determine which trial method is fairest for both parties involved.

As you enter the chatroom, you will notice that there is no chat bar. This is because the judge or admin will unblock the chat bar when it is your turn to speak, so everyone gets a turn to respond. You will then have the opportunity to enter a text response, multiple choice response, or raise your hand.
